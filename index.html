Automated Sarcoma Grading System

Using Deep Learning for the Automated Veterinary Soft Tissue Sarcoma Grading of Perivascular Wall Tumours

Taran Rai1, Ambra Morisi2, Barbara Bacci2, Spencer Angus Thomas3, Miroslaw Bober1, Roberto La Ragione2, Kevin Wells1
1Centre for Vision, Speech and Signal Processing, University of Surrey, 2School for Veterinary Medicine, University of Surrey 3National Physical Laboratory
Paper arXiv Video Code

D-NeRF turns selfie videos from your phone into free-viewpoint portraits (nerfies).

Abstract

We present the first method capable of photorealistically reconstructing a non-rigidly deforming scene using photos/videos captured casually from mobile phones.

Our approach -- D-NeRF -- augments neural radiance fields

We show that D-NeRF can turn casually captured selfie photos/videos into deformable NeRF models that allow for photorealistic renderings of the subject from arbitrary viewpoints, which we dub "nerfies". We evaluate our method by collecting data using a rig with two mobile phones that take time-synchronized photos, yielding train/validation images of the same pose at different viewpoints. We show that our method faithfully reconstructs non-rigidly deforming scenes and reproduces unseen views with high fidelity.
